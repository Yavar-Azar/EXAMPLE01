Spin-orbit coupling in halide perovskite¶

Types of SOC¶

there are two types of SOC in solids:

  1. Symmetry-independent
    exists in all types of crystals stem from SOI in atomic orbitals
    > here we can compare splitting in 6p(Pb) and 5p(I) orbitals

  2. Symmetry-dependent exists only in crystals without inversion symmetry

  1. Dresselhaus interaction (bulk): Bulk-Induced-Assymetry (BIA)

  2. Bychkov-Rashba (surface): Surface-Induced-Asymmetry (SIA)

“Distinction between Rashba and Dresselhaus effects can be envisioned by con- sidering a quasi-2D structure (in-plane: (x, y)) having C\(_2v\) symmetry and limiting the expression of the linear terms. Then, the Rashba–Dresselhaus Hamiltonian reads:

\(H_{RD}(k_\parallel ) = \lambda_R (k_x\sigma_y − k_y\sigma_x) + \lambda_D (k_x\sigma_x − k_y\sigma_y )\)

where k\(_\parallel\)= (\(k_x , k_y\) ), \(\sigma_x\) and \(\sigma_y\) are the in-plane components of the Pauli spin matrices, \(\lambda_R\) and \(\lambda_D\) the Rashba and Dresselhaus parameters, characteristic of the strength of respective effects.”:cite:`undefined`
